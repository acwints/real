## ‚ö†Ô∏è Critical Gaps & Risks (Where You're Off)

### 1. **SCALE MISMATCH - Biggest Risk**

**The Problem:**
- Your project: **750-1,200 units**
- Largest comp: **302 units (Aya)**
- **You're proposing 2.5x-4x larger than anything comparable**

**Why This Matters:**
- **Absorption Risk**: Can you lease up 750-1,200 units? Largest comp is 302. No precedent for this scale in Berkeley.
- **Construction Risk**: Larger projects = longer timelines = more cost escalation risk
- **Market Risk**: Flooding the market with 1,200 units could depress rents
- **Operational Complexity**: Managing 1,200 units is exponentially harder than 300

**Reality Check:**
- Most successful TOD projects in Bay Area: **200-400 units**
- Even large developments typically phase: 300-400 units per phase
- **Recommendation**: Consider phasing (Phase 1: 400-500 units, Phase 2: 300-400 units)

### 2. **PRICING ASSUMPTIONS MAY BE OPTIMISTIC**

**The Problem:**
- Essay mentions $3,900/month for 2BR (~800 SF) = **$4.88/SF/month**
- Comps show price/SF ranging from **$237-$481** (these are SALE prices, not rent)
- **Missing**: Actual rent/SF data from comps

**Gap Analysis:**
- If comps sold at $237-$481/SF, and assuming 5% cap rate:
  - $237/SF √ó 5% = $11.85/SF/year = **$0.99/SF/month** (way below your $4.88)
  - $481/SF √ó 5% = $24.05/SF/year = **$2.00/SF/month** (still below)
- **Your rent assumption ($4.88/SF/month) implies a sale price of ~$1,170/SF** - that's **2.4x higher than the highest comp**

**Reality Check:**
- Either your rent assumptions are too high, OR
- The comps are undervalued/older stock, OR  
- You're assuming significant rent growth that hasn't materialized yet

**Recommendation**: 
- Validate rent/SF from actual comps
- If $4.88/SF/month is correct, explain why (new construction premium, amenities, etc.)
- If not, adjust pro forma to realistic rents

### 3. **CONSTRUCTION COSTS NOT ADDRESSED**

**The Problem:**
- Essay mentions "construction costs remain elevated" but provides **no specific numbers**
- No cost per unit or cost per SF estimates
- No comparison to comp construction costs

**Reality Check:**
- Bay Area construction costs: **$400-600/SF** for multifamily (2024)
- For 750 units at 800 SF avg = 600,000 SF √ó $500/SF = **$300M just in construction**
- Plus land, soft costs, financing = **$400-500M total project cost**

**Gap:**
- If total cost is $400M and you sell at $400K/unit (750 units) = $300M
- **You're underwater by $100M**

**Recommendation**: 
- Provide detailed construction cost estimates
- Show how you achieve cost efficiency at scale
- Validate that revenue can cover costs

### 4. **LAND COSTS UNCLEAR**

**The Problem:**
- Essay mentions "BART joint-development revenue-sharing" but **no specific terms**
- Ground lease vs. purchase? What's the land cost?
- "Below-market ground-lease terms" - how much below market?

**Reality Check:**
- 4.4 acres in Berkeley near BART = **$5-10M+** if purchased
- Ground lease: typically 3-8% of land value annually
- If land value is $8M, ground lease = $240K-$640K/year
- Over 30 years = $7.2M-$19.2M in lease payments

**Gap:**
- Need specific land cost structure
- How does "below-market" translate to actual dollars?
- What's the impact on project economics?

**Recommendation**: 
- Quantify land costs explicitly
- Show how BART partnership improves economics
- Compare to market-rate land costs

### 5. **MIXED-USE RETAIL ASSUMPTIONS**

**The Problem:**
- Essay mentions "ground-floor retail" but **no revenue projections**
- East Bay office vacancy is **13.5%** (elevated)
- Retail is struggling post-COVID

**Reality Check:**
- Retail rents: $30-60/SF/year in Berkeley
- If 20,000 SF retail √ó $45/SF = $900K/year
- That's **<1% of total revenue** if residential is $30M+/year
- Retail adds complexity, not significant value

**Gap:**
- Retail revenue is minimal relative to residential
- Retail adds construction cost and operational complexity
- Is it worth it?

**Recommendation**: 
- Quantify retail revenue vs. cost
- Consider if retail is necessary or just "nice to have"
- Maybe reduce retail footprint to minimize risk

### 6. **ENTITLEMENT RISK UNDERESTIMATED**

**The Problem:**
- Essay acknowledges Berkeley's "detailed review processes" but seems optimistic
- Mentions "Ashby Flea Market" and "long-standing community institutions"
- **No specific community engagement plan or timeline**

**Reality Check:**
- Berkeley entitlement: **2-5 years** for large projects
- Community opposition is likely (NIMBY concerns)
- Environmental review (CEQA) can be lengthy
- **Risk of delays = cost escalation**

**Gap:**
- Need realistic entitlement timeline
- Need community engagement strategy
- Need contingency for delays

**Recommendation**: 
- Add 2-3 year entitlement timeline to pro forma
- Budget for community benefits/agreements
- Include delay contingencies

### 7. **CAPITAL STRUCTURE VAGUE**

**The Problem:**
- Essay mentions "senior debt, sponsor equity, grants, mission-aligned LPs"
- **No specific amounts, terms, or sources**
- "Mission-aligned capital" sounds good but may not exist

**Reality Check:**
- Typical capital stack: 60-70% debt, 30-40% equity
- For $400M project: $240-280M debt, $120-160M equity
- **Where does $120-160M equity come from?**
- Grants are competitive and limited
- "Mission-aligned LPs" may have different return expectations

**Gap:**
- Need specific capital sources
- Need realistic return expectations
- Need to show equity is actually available

**Recommendation**: 
- Identify specific equity sources
- Show letters of interest or commitments
- Quantify grant availability
- Be realistic about "mission-aligned" capital

### 8. **ABSORPTION ASSUMPTIONS**

**The Problem:**
- Essay mentions "strong pre-leasing" and "rapid stabilization"
- **No specific absorption timeline**
- 750-1,200 units is a lot to lease up

**Reality Check:**
- Typical absorption: 20-40 units/month for large projects
- 750 units √∑ 30 units/month = **25 months** (2+ years)
- 1,200 units √∑ 30 units/month = **40 months** (3+ years)
- During lease-up, you're paying debt service with partial occupancy

**Gap:**
- Need realistic absorption timeline
- Need lease-up financing (bridge loan?)
- Need to model cash flow during lease-up

**Recommendation**: 
- Provide specific absorption assumptions
- Model lease-up period cash flows
- Include lease-up financing in capital stack
